Push Day:

As the name suggests a push day involes pushing movements either above you, from you, or below you, the primary muscle groups involved in a push day include the chest, shoulders and also the triceps. In this page you will find 3 simple but effective exercises that will help you get started for an efficient but intense workout!

1. Bench Press

The bench press is one of the most iconic movements of bodybuilding, it is the primary push day movement. It uses your chest, shoulders and triceps to push the bar from the bottom of your chest and all the way back up. It is typically performed on a flat bench using a barbell or dumbbells.

Tips:

  • Have a friend spot you if you are going heavy.
  • Ensure your feet are planted firmly on the ground.
  • Bring the bar all the way down to touch your chest and lock out your elbows.

2. Shoulder Press

The Shoulder press can be performed standing up or using a bench for support, and will primarily utilise the front delts. To perform the shoulder press bring the weights to your shoulders and push up and lock out the elbows.

Tips:

  • Ensure back is straight and firmly against the bench.
  • Avoid going to heavy as shoulder injuries are the most common in body building.
  • Keep a tight core to focus on mind-muscle connection in shoulder muscles.

3. Tricep Dips

The Tricep dip is an easy to perform exercise as you only need to use your body weight and requires minimal equipment. All you need is somewhere to rest your hands on that can support your body weight. Furthermore it targets all 3 heads of the tricep.

Tips:

  • Keep elbows tucked in to avoid injuring the shoulder.
  • Extend your legs or rest them on a chair infront of you to increase the diffuculty.
  • Keep hands near your side to enhance tricep activation.
